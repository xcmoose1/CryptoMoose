Crypto Fundamentals - Wallets & Security

2. Understanding Crypto Wallets & Security

⚠️ Security Warning

Never share your private keys or seed phrases with anyone. Keep them offline and in a secure location.

Hot Wallets

Connected to the internet for easy access.

Examples:

  • MetaMask
  • Trust Wallet
  • Coinbase Wallet

Best for: Daily transactions, small amounts

Cold Wallets

Offline storage for maximum security.

Examples:

  • Ledger Nano
  • Trezor
  • Paper Wallets

Best for: Long-term storage, large amounts

Multi-Signature Wallets

Require multiple approvals for transactions.

Features:

  • Enhanced security
  • Team management
  • Inheritance planning

Best for: Business accounts, shared funds

Essential Security Practices

Common Security Threats

  • Phishing Attacks: Fake websites and emails mimicking legitimate services
  • Malware: Software designed to steal private keys
  • Social Engineering: Manipulative tactics to gain access to your funds
  • Clipboard Hijacking: Malware that changes copied wallet addresses
